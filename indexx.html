Design Beginnings


Design Fuego

This project utilized the application Visual Code Studio to adjust a code to make a presentation that introduced ourself.

Logo A/B/C

With the help of the design tools Canva and Adobe Spark, we created three logos that represented us as indivduals.

UX Testing Logo Project

For this project we created a UX research project where we interviewed subjects to get their opinions on our logo designs. We then used this feedback to redesign our logos.

Cartoon Portrait

We used Adobe Illustrator to make a one panel comic of ourselves.

Good Design

We had to select a design that we thought was good. We then created a document to explain to an audience what elements made this design good.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

We let our design resources and technology assist us in our designing, but added our own personal touches to each project.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

We used a variety of design tools and applications. This gave us a range of design methods to utlize. We additionally read books and sources to increase our knowledge of design principles and implement them into our designs, such as alignment, text, color combinations, etc.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

During each class meeting, we worked on one of these projects and as we learned these new skills and knowledge as a class we were able to ask each other questions and share information with one another. We shared what we thought worked well in our designs and what needed each revision.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

By looking at what others in class created and recieving feedback, we were able to come up with ways to finalize our projects and portray them in our portfolio.

5. Learn to present your work.

We presented these projects during our class meetings to share our work with others and gain positive and negative feedback from one another. We formed our projects in a way that was easily accesible to our peers.
